Field Notes #1: Why I'm Starting Now

Posted December 15, 2025 • Oregon City, Oregon

Mission: CBA Digital strengthens small local businesses by improving operations and digital visibility.

Thriving neighbors. Smarter operations + digital visibility.

There’s something powerful about deciding to finally start. I’ve waited longer than I should have, but in that time I’ve learned — about technology, about process, and about what it really means to turn ideas into tangible results. This isn’t just about business; it’s about rediscovering that drive to create, to lead, to bring something of value into the world.

Entrepreneurship has always fascinated me. I’ve watched people take that leap — open a shop, launch a service, stake their livelihood on an idea — and I’ve always admired the courage it takes to bet on yourself. That’s the spirit that built communities like ours. It’s the heartbeat of every Main Street.

For me, that Main Street is Oregon City. It’s a place where history meets possibility, where the old and new blend together. Every storefront tells part of a story, and together they create something much larger — a community of people who believe in their city and want to see it thrive. That’s the community I want to contribute to, not just as a resident but as someone who helps business owners build, improve, and succeed.

My mission is simple: to help small businesses work smarter, grow stronger, and create lasting local impact. Through consulting, I aim to bridge the gap between ideas and execution — helping entrepreneurs use technology and sound processes to open doors, create opportunities, and strengthen their foundations. Whether it’s in Oregon City or beyond, I believe that small business is the engine of vitality in every community.

This blog is where I’ll share what I learn along the way — lessons about growth, about building better systems, and about staying true to a mission. It’s also where I’ll celebrate the people who make communities like ours special: those who dream, start, and persist. Every town has a soul, and on Oregon City’s Main Street, that soul shines through its small business owners.

This is the journey I’m choosing to document — not just building a business, but helping others do the same. One step, one conversation, one idea at a time.
