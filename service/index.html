Business Snapshot & Clarity Audit

Stop guessing what to fix.

The Business Snapshot is a focused assessment that shows how work actually flows through your business — where it breaks down, why the owner gets pulled back in, and what to fix next.

Request a Snapshot What you’ll get

Designed for owner-led service businesses that feel busy, inconsistent, or overly dependent on the owner.

This is for you if:

  • Work moves, but outcomes aren’t predictable
  • Handoffs break and problems come back to you
  • You’re involved in too many day-to-day decisions
  • Your online presence doesn’t reflect how you actually operate

What the Snapshot solves

Most businesses don’t lack effort — they lack visibility and prioritization.

The problem

Operations grow organically. Tools pile up. Responsibilities blur. The owner becomes the safety net when things break.

The approach

We map reality — how work enters, moves, and completes — then identify where breakdowns, rework, and dependency originate.

The outcome

Clear priorities, cleaner handoffs, fewer fires, and a grounded plan for the next 30–60 days.

Deliverables

What you’ll receive

Concise, decision-ready outputs — not a bloated report.

Before

  • Short intake questionnaire
  • Review of your website and local visibility basics
  • High-level overview of tools, roles, and workflows

Working session

  • 60–90 minute live working session
  • Map how work actually flows today
  • Surface handoff failures and owner pull-back points

After

  • Written summary in plain language
  • Top 3–5 priorities ranked by impact
  • 30–60 day action plan with clear sequencing

Optional next step

  • Time-boxed Tune-Up for implementation support
  • No obligation — the Snapshot stands on its own

How this fits into a larger engagement

The Snapshot is the front door — not a sales trap.

Many clients use the Snapshot to realign internally and implement changes on their own.

Others choose a short Tune-Up or light ongoing support to stabilize execution. You decide after you see the priorities.
